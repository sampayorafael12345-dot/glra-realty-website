For Buyers: Documents You Need to Check

📄 Title Deed (Most Important!)

Confirms legal ownership of the property. Must show the seller's name as the registered owner. Check for any liens or encumbrances.

🔒 Encumbrance Certificate

Proves the property is free from legal or monetary liabilities. Shows no unpaid loans or mortgages attached to the property.

💰 Tax Declaration & Receipts

Confirms property taxes are paid up to date. Unpaid taxes become the buyer's responsibility. Ask for receipts from the past 3-5 years.

🏗️ Approved Building Plan

Ensures construction followed legal permits. Check if any renovations were properly authorized.

✅ Occupancy Permit

Certifies the property is safe for living. Issued by the local building official. Required for home loan applications.

🗺️ Survey Plan

Shows exact boundaries of the property. Identifies easements or right-of-way issues. Prevents neighbor disputes.

Property Disclosure Checklist for Sellers

Be honest about these items - it protects you legally:

  • Any structural defects (cracks, leaks, foundation issues)
  • Pest infestations (termites, rodents, etc.)
  • Past flooding or water damage
  • Unauthorized renovations or constructions
  • Boundary disputes with neighbors
  • Pending legal cases involving the property
  • Homeowner's Association (HOA) issues or unpaid dues
  • Easements or right-of-way access by others
  • Plans for nearby development that may affect value
Important: Failure to disclose material defects can lead to legal action after the sale.
